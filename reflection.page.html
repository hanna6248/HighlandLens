My Reflection on Living and Studying in Baguio

Since I was young, I had always dreamt of studying in Baguio. Something about the city — the cool air, the pine trees, the endless creativity around every corner — captured my heart even before I set foot here. When the time finally came for me to move and study, I knew it was the start of a chapter I had been waiting for all my life. Becoming a student at University of Baguio (UB) felt like a dream come true. I quickly fell in love with the campus, the lively atmosphere, and the way the city seemed to embrace students like me. UB became more than just a school — it became a second home. Here, I found friends who shared the same ambitions, struggles, and laughter, people who turned from classmates into lifelong companions. My life in Baguio wasn’t just about academics. It was also about experiencing the spirit of the city through its many beautiful places. Laser was one of the first places I visited — a lively spot that captured the youthful, vibrant energy of Baguio nights. Victoria became a familiar name too, a place where errands and little adventures blended together into my daily routine. After long days of classes and studying, I often found myself looking for quiet corners to breathe and reflect. Cafe Gia was one of my favorites — the aroma of coffee, the cozy ambiance, and the gentle hum of conversations made it the perfect place to unwind with a book or a journal. Whenever I needed a spark of creativity, I would wander through Ili-Likha Artists' Village, a colorful haven of art and nature hidden among the trees. It reminded me that creativity and learning go hand in hand. There were days when my friends and I would treat ourselves to something special. We loved eating at Oh My Gulay, an artist-owned vegetarian restaurant perched above Session Road. Sitting there, with Baguio’s skyline stretched out before us, we would dream out loud about our futures. The Grumpy Pig was another favorite, especially when we needed a comforting meal after a tough week. Faith also played a role in my journey. I found peace visiting a nearby church, where the quiet allowed me to pray, reflect, and thank God for bringing me to Baguio, for my education, and for the people I was blessed to meet. Of course, exploring Baguio wouldn’t be complete without the magic of its local culture. Strolling through Pasakalye on weekends was always an adventure — discovering local art, music, handmade crafts, and meeting artists passionate about their work made me feel like I was part of something much bigger than myself. I also loved spending time at Bookends, a small but charming bookstore where every visit felt like a treasure hunt. It was easy to lose track of time surrounded by the scent of old pages and stories waiting to be read. One of the most memorable places for me was Ong Koleyt, where creativity ran wild and every piece of art spoke of freedom, passion, and resilience — traits I came to associate with Baguio itself. Throughout my stay, what made everything even more special were the friendships I formed. I met people from different backgrounds and walks of life, and together we shared countless memories — from late-night study sessions to spontaneous food trips and endless conversations under the pine trees. We supported each other through exams, homesickness, and personal milestones. They made Baguio feel less like a city and more like a family. Looking back, my time in Baguio has been about so much more than studying. It’s about growth, about finding pieces of myself I never knew were missing. Every street I walked, every new place I visited, and every friendship I formed shaped who I am today. I will always be grateful to Baguio, to UB, and to every person and place that made my dream not just a reality, but a beautiful, unforgettable journey.
