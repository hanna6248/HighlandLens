Pasakalye

Just a few steps away from Bookends, there’s a little artistic gem called Pasakalye—a vibrant marketplace that showcases the creativity of local artists and artisans. Every time I visit, I can’t help but be drawn to the colorful display of handmade accessories, unique artworks, and beautifully crafted nail extensions.
One of the things I love most about Pasakalye is its celebration of craftsmanship. The bracelets and necklaces sold here aren’t mass-produced; they are carefully handwoven and designed, making each piece one of a kind. I always find joy in picking out a bracelet or two, knowing that I’m not just buying an accessory—I’m supporting local artisans and their passion for their craft.
Another thing that makes Pasakalye special is the variety of handmade nail extensions. These aren’t your typical store-bought designs; each set is hand-painted with intricate details, giving them a unique and artistic touch. Whether I’m looking for something simple and elegant or bold and eye-catching, there’s always a design that suits my style.
Aside from accessories and nail art, the place is also filled with beautiful paintings, sketches, and other handmade crafts. It’s like walking through an open-air art gallery, where every stall tells a story through colors and textures. Whether it’s a small postcard-sized painting or a larger framed artwork, there’s always something that catches my eye.
What I love most about Pasakalye is the feeling of community. The artists and sellers are always warm and welcoming, eager to share the stories behind their creations. It’s a place where creativity thrives, where every purchase feels meaningful, and where I always leave with something special—whether it’s a new bracelet, a piece of art, or simply the inspiration to create something of my own.
If you love handmade crafts, unique accessories, and supporting local artists, Pasakalye is definitely a must-visit. Every time I go, I find something new to love, and that’s why it remains one of my favorite spots in Baguio. Come visit Pasakalye through my lenses!
